04 Our Story

Two pairs of hands,
one stone oven,
not enough sleep.

We started TAB in a small unit off Pilgrim Street in spring 2024. The plan was simple: bake the kind of sourdough we couldn’t find anywhere else in Newcastle, and sell it on Sundays at the Quayside.

Two years later we’ve baked over forty thousand loaves between us, and the queue at the Sunday market still surprises us every week. The subscription box was a customer’s idea — they said they were tired of always missing the good loaves before noon. So we put one aside for them. Then ten people. Then five hundred.

This website is the next step. Same bread, same hands, same hours. Just an easier way for you to get a loaf onto your table on a Saturday morning.

— Eli & Mae

A real sourdough is at its best for three to four days at room temperature — keep it cut-side down on the board, no plastic. After that, slice it and freeze it. Toasted from frozen is genuinely as good as fresh.

All our sourdough loaves are vegan (flour, water, salt, wild yeast, that’s the lot). The viennoiserie contains butter and dairy. Filter for ‘Vegan only’ in the subscription configurator if you want a fully plant-based box.
Allergen information is on every product page in line with FSA guidance. We bake with wheat, rye and dairy in the same kitchen, so we cannot guarantee any product is gluten-free or nut-free, even where ingredients are. If you’re unsure, get in touch before ordering.
